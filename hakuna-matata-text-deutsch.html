What Does "Hakuna Matata" Mean in German?

The phrase "Hakuna Matata" is actually a Swahili phrase that means "no problem" or "no worries". However, in the German language, it is translated as "Sorgenfrei" which also means "carefree". So, if you want to use the phrase in a German context, you can say "Sorgenfrei" instead of "Hakuna Matata".

If you want to use the phrase "Hakuna Matata" in a German sentence, you can say "Es ist alles sorgenfrei" which means "everything is carefree" or "keine Sorgen" which means "no worries". You can also use it in casual conversations with friends and family to express a carefree attitude.
