French Term for a Sketch Crossword

It's 2023 and the world of crossword puzzles is still going strong. One particular type of crossword that has gained popularity in recent years is the sketch crossword. But did you know that this type of crossword actually has a French name?

In French, a sketch crossword is called "Mots Croisés Dessinés." This directly translates to "crosswords drawn" or "sketched crosswords." It's a fitting name, as the puzzles often require the solver to draw or sketch out the answers in the grid.

The concept of sketch crosswords is not new, but it has gained a new following in recent years. These puzzles often feature a grid with numbered squares, similar to a regular crossword. However, instead of clues, each square contains a small picture or symbol that represents a word or phrase. The solver must then draw or sketch out the answer in the corresponding square.

How to Solve a Sketch Crossword

Solving a sketch crossword requires a bit of creativity and imagination. Instead of relying solely on words and their definitions, the solver must be able to interpret the visual clues in each square. Here are a few tips to help you solve a sketch crossword:

  1. Start with the easiest squares - Look for squares that have obvious visual clues and fill them in first.
  2. Use context clues - Sometimes a picture may not be immediately obvious, but looking at the surrounding squares can provide context and help you figure out the answer.
  3. Think outside the box - Don't be afraid to think creatively and come up with alternative interpretations of the visual clues.

The History of Sketch Crosswords

Sketch crosswords have been around for decades, but they have undergone a resurgence in popularity in recent years. Some credit the rise of social media and online puzzle communities for the renewed interest in this type of crossword.

One of the earliest examples of a sketch crossword can be traced back to the 1930s, when British puzzle enthusiast Leonard Dawe created a series of puzzles for his students. These puzzles featured a grid with pictures instead of words, and the students had to fill in the corresponding words.

Since then, sketch crosswords have appeared in various forms in puzzle books, newspapers, and online. They continue to challenge and entertain puzzle lovers around the world.

Famous Sketch Crossword Puzzles

There have been many notable sketch crosswords throughout history, but perhaps the most famous is the one created by Leonard Dawe during World War II. This puzzle, which was published in The Daily Telegraph in 1944, contained a series of seemingly random words and phrases that were later revealed to be code words for the D-Day invasion.

Other famous sketch crosswords include the "Picasso Crossword," which featured drawings by the famous artist, and the "Sistine Chapel Crossword," which featured drawings of famous scenes from the Sistine Chapel ceiling.

The Future of Sketch Crosswords

As puzzle enthusiasts continue to seek out new and exciting challenges, it's likely that sketch crosswords will continue to gain popularity. With the rise of technology, it's also possible that we will see new and innovative ways of incorporating visual clues into crossword puzzles. Whatever the future holds, one thing is certain - the world of crossword puzzles will continue to evolve and captivate puzzle lovers for years to come.
