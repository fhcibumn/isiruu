How much soup per person?

There's nothing like a warm bowl of soup on a chilly day. But when you're cooking for a group, it can be difficult to know how much soup to make. Nobody wants to run out of soup, but you also don't want to have a ton of leftovers. So, how much soup per person is the right amount?

The Rule of Thumb

The general rule of thumb is to plan for about 1 cup of soup per person. However, this can vary depending on a few factors. If you're serving soup as a main course, you may want to plan for a bit more. On the other hand, if you're serving soup as a side dish, you can plan for a bit less.

Another factor to consider is the type of soup you're serving. A thick and hearty soup, like a chowder or stew, will likely fill people up more than a lighter soup, like a broth-based soup. Keep this in mind when determining how much soup per person to plan for.

Lastly, consider the appetites of your guests. If you're serving a group of big eaters, you may want to plan for a bit more soup per person. On the other hand, if you're serving a group of light eaters, you can plan for a bit less.

Calculating the Amount of Soup

If you're still unsure about how much soup per person to plan for, you can use a simple calculation to help determine the amount. Multiply the number of guests you'll be serving by the desired serving size (usually around 1 cup). This will give you the total amount of soup you'll need to make.

For example, if you're serving 10 people and want to plan for 1 cup of soup per person, you'll need to make 10 cups of soup.

Leftovers

While it's important to plan for enough soup for your guests, it's also important to consider leftovers. Soup can be a great meal to have on hand for lunches throughout the week or to freeze for future meals. Keep this in mind when determining how much soup per person to plan for. It's better to have a bit too much soup than not enough!

In Conclusion

When it comes to how much soup per person to plan for, a general rule of thumb is around 1 cup of soup. However, there are a few factors to consider, such as the type of soup, the appetites of your guests, and whether you want leftovers. With these factors in mind, you can calculate the amount of soup you'll need to make for your group. Happy cooking!
