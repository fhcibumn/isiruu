A Guide to the Wettest Months in the Sunshine State

Florida is known for its sunny beaches and warm weather, but it's also home to a rainy season that can bring heavy downpours and thunderstorms. If you're planning a trip to Florida, it's important to know when the rainy season starts and ends, so you can pack accordingly and plan your activities around the weather.

When Does the Rainy Season in Florida Start?

The rainy season in Florida typically starts in late May or early June and lasts until October. During this time, the state experiences a shift in weather patterns, with increased humidity, daily thunderstorms, and occasional tropical storms or hurricanes. The peak of the rainy season usually occurs in August and September, when the state can receive up to 7 inches of rain per month.

What Should You Expect During the Rainy Season?

If you're visiting Florida during the rainy season, be prepared for sudden rain showers that can last anywhere from a few minutes to a few hours. Thunderstorms are common, and can bring lightning and strong winds. It's also important to note that Florida's beaches may experience dangerous rip currents during this time, so be sure to check the weather forecast and heed any warnings from lifeguards.

How Can You Stay Safe During the Rainy Season?

To stay safe during the rainy season in Florida, be sure to pack rain gear such as a waterproof jacket, umbrella, and sturdy shoes. If you're driving, be aware of flooded areas and never attempt to drive through standing water. If you're caught outside during a thunderstorm, seek shelter indoors or in a car, and avoid open fields, beaches, and tall trees.

What Are Some Fun Activities to Do During the Rainy Season?

While the rainy season in Florida may put a damper on some outdoor activities, there are still plenty of fun things to do during this time. Visit one of Florida's many museums or indoor attractions, such as the Kennedy Space Center or the Salvador Dali Museum. Take a cooking class or visit a local brewery. Or, embrace the rain and go on a scenic drive or hike through one of Florida's state parks.

Now that you know when the rainy season in Florida starts and ends, and how to stay safe and have fun during this time, you're ready to enjoy all that the Sunshine State has to offer.
