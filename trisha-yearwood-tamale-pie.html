Trisha Yearwood Tamale Pie

Trisha Yearwood is a renowned country music artist, television personality, and cookbook author. Her passion for cooking is reflected in her recipes, and one of her most famous dishes is the Tamale Pie. This savory and spicy dish is a delicious twist on the classic cornbread and chili combination, and it's perfect for a cozy night in or a fun family gathering.

The Tamale Pie is a casserole dish made with a layer of cornbread on the bottom, followed by a layer of spicy chili, and topped with a layer of melted cheese. The cornbread layer is made from a combination of cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, milk, eggs, and melted butter. The chili layer is made with ground beef, onions, garlic, canned tomatoes, canned beans, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. The cheese layer is made with shredded cheddar cheese.

To make the Tamale Pie, start by preheating the oven to 375 degrees F. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium-high heat until browned. Add the onions and garlic, and cook until softened. Stir in the canned tomatoes, beans, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper, and let simmer for 10 minutes. In a separate bowl, mix together the cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. In another bowl, whisk together the milk, eggs, and melted butter. Combine the wet and dry ingredients, and pour the mixture into a greased 9x13 inch baking dish. Spoon the chili over the cornbread mixture, and sprinkle with shredded cheese. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese

Variations:

Vegetarian Tamale Pie:

To make a vegetarian version of the Tamale Pie, simply replace the ground beef with a meat substitute such as crumbled tofu or vegetarian ground beef. You can also add more vegetables to the chili layer, such as bell peppers, zucchini, or corn.

Spicy Tamale Pie:

If you like your food spicy, you can add more chili powder or cayenne pepper to the chili layer. You can also top the Tamale Pie with sliced jalapenos or a drizzle of hot sauce.

Cheesy Tamale Pie:

If you're a cheese lover, you can add more shredded cheese to the top of the Tamale Pie. You can also mix in different types of cheese, such as Monterey Jack or pepper jack, for a more complex flavor.

Individual Tamale Pies:

If you want to make individual servings of the Tamale Pie, you can use muffin tins instead of a large baking dish. Simply spoon the cornbread mixture into the bottom of each muffin cup, top with the chili, and sprinkle with cheese. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
