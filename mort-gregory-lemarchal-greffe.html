What is Cystic Fibrosis?

Cystic fibrosis is a genetic disorder that affects the lungs, pancreas, and other organs. It causes the production of thick, sticky mucus that clogs the airways and makes it difficult to breathe. It also affects the digestive system, making it difficult to digest food and absorb nutrients.

The life expectancy of someone with cystic fibrosis has improved in recent years thanks to medical advancements, but it remains a serious and life-threatening condition.
