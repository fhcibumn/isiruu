Chicago Bears Undrafted Free Agents 2022

The Chicago Bears have had a long history of finding hidden talent in the undrafted free agent market. In 2022, the Bears once again scoured the country for potential diamonds in the rough. Here are some of the undrafted free agents that the Bears signed in 2022 and how they might impact the team.

1. Chris Garrett, DE, Concordia St. Paul

Chris Garrett was a dominant force at the Division II level, racking up 36 sacks in his college career. Despite his impressive production, he went undrafted and signed with the Bears. Garrett has a chance to make an immediate impact as a situational pass rusher. His explosiveness off the edge and ability to convert speed to power make him a potential steal for the Bears.

2. Bryce Thompson, CB, Tennessee

Bryce Thompson was considered a mid-round prospect by many draft analysts, but he ended up going undrafted. The Bears were quick to scoop him up, though, and they may have landed a starting-caliber cornerback. Thompson has good size, speed, and ball skills, and he was a playmaker in college. He'll have to prove himself against NFL competition, but the talent is there.

3. Micah Simon, WR, BYU

Micah Simon was a productive receiver at BYU, but he was overshadowed by teammate Dax Milne in the draft process. Simon went undrafted, but the Bears gave him a chance to compete for a roster spot. Simon is a smooth route runner with good hands and solid athleticism. He'll have to show that he can separate from NFL cornerbacks, but he's a sleeper to make the team.

4. Darius Stills, DT, West Virginia

Darius Stills was a disruptive force at West Virginia, but he fell through the cracks in the draft due to concerns about his size and athleticism. The Bears saw something in him, though, and they signed him as an undrafted free agent. Stills has a quick first step and uses his leverage well to penetrate the backfield. He'll have to prove that he can hold up against NFL offensive linemen, but he has the potential to be a rotational player.
