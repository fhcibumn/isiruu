Top 10 Percent Net Worth By Age: How Do You Compare?

As we enter 2023, many of us may be wondering where we stand financially compared to others our age. It's common to feel like we're not saving enough or investing wisely, but understanding the net worth of others in our age group can provide some perspective. In this article, we'll explore the top 10 percent net worth by age and what it means for your financial goals.

Age 18-24

For those just starting out, the top 10 percent net worth by age 18-24 is around $100,000. This may seem like a lofty goal, but it's important to remember that many in this age range are still in school or just beginning their careers. The key to building wealth at this age is to focus on saving and investing early, even if it's just small amounts.

One way to boost your net worth is to take advantage of employer-sponsored retirement plans, such as a 401(k) or IRA. Contributing even a small percentage of your income can add up over time thanks to compound interest. Additionally, living below your means and avoiding debt can help you build a solid foundation for future financial success.

Age 25-34

By age 25-34, the top 10 percent net worth jumps to around $350,000. This age range is typically when people are settling into their careers and may be starting families. It's important to balance saving for retirement with other financial goals, such as saving for a down payment on a house or paying off student loans.

One way to boost your net worth at this age is to focus on earning more through career advancement or side hustles. Additionally, consider investing in stocks or real estate to diversify your portfolio and potentially earn higher returns. It's also important to have a solid emergency fund in case of unexpected expenses, such as medical bills or job loss.

Age 35-44

By age 35-44, the top 10 percent net worth jumps even higher to around $1 million. This age range is when people may be hitting their peak earning years and may have more disposable income to save and invest. However, this is also a time when people may be juggling multiple financial priorities, such as saving for retirement, paying for their children's education, and caring for aging parents.

To boost your net worth at this age, it's important to stay disciplined with your saving and investing. Consider working with a financial advisor to create a plan that balances your various financial goals. Additionally, continue to live below your means and avoid lifestyle inflation, which can eat into your savings. Finally, make sure you have adequate insurance coverage to protect your assets and loved ones.

Age 45-54

By age 45-54, the top 10 percent net worth jumps to around $2.5 million. This age range is when people are starting to think seriously about retirement and may be looking to downsize their homes or shift their investment strategies. It's important to have a solid retirement plan in place that takes into account factors such as Social Security benefits, healthcare costs, and inflation.

To boost your net worth at this age, consider working with a financial planner to create a retirement income strategy that balances your various sources of income. Additionally, continue to save aggressively and consider investing in alternative assets such as private equity or real estate. Finally, make sure you have a solid estate plan in place to protect your assets and ensure they're distributed according to your wishes.
