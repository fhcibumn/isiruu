Vivono a Nord dei Milanesi - A Look at Life in Northern Milan

Introduction

When it comes to Milan, most people think of the fashion, the food, and the bustling streets of the city center. However, there's more to Milan than just what's in the city center. In fact, many people live in the suburbs and outskirts of Milan, including those who live to the north of the city. In this article, we'll take a closer look at what life is like for those who "vivono a nord dei milanesi" - those who live in the north of Milan.

The Neighborhoods

One of the first things to note about the northern suburbs of Milan is that they are quite diverse. From the upscale and luxurious neighborhoods of Cernusco sul Naviglio and Cinisello Balsamo, to the more working-class areas like Bresso and Sesto San Giovanni, there is something for everyone in the north of Milan.

Many of these neighborhoods are also quite green, with plenty of parks and open spaces for residents to enjoy. For example, the Parco Nord is a large park in the north of Milan that covers over 600 hectares and is a popular spot for jogging, cycling, and picnicking.

Overall, the neighborhoods in the north of Milan are known for being safe, family-friendly, and well-connected to the city center via public transportation.

The Food

When it comes to food, Milan is known for its delicious cuisine, and the north of the city is no exception. One of the most popular dishes in the north of Milan is "risotto alla milanese", a creamy and flavorful rice dish made with saffron and Parmesan cheese. Another local specialty is "cotoletta alla milanese", a breaded and fried veal cutlet that is often served with a side of arugula and cherry tomatoes.

In addition to these local specialties, the north of Milan is also home to many international and fusion restaurants, reflecting the diversity of the neighborhoods. Whether you're in the mood for sushi, pizza, or a hearty plate of pasta, you're sure to find something to suit your tastes in the north of Milan.

The Lifestyle

Life in the north of Milan is generally quite laid-back and relaxed compared to the hustle and bustle of the city center. With plenty of open spaces and parks, many residents enjoy spending time outdoors, whether it's for a picnic, a jog, or a game of soccer. The neighborhoods are also known for having a strong sense of community, with many local events and festivals throughout the year where residents can come together and celebrate.

While the nightlife in the north of Milan may not be as vibrant as in the city center, there are still plenty of options for those looking for a fun night out. Many of the neighborhoods have their own bars and pubs, and there are also several clubs and music venues in the area.

Overall, the lifestyle in the north of Milan is perfect for those looking for a balance between city living and suburban tranquility.

The Economy

Finally, it's worth noting that the north of Milan is also an important economic hub for the region. Many international companies have their headquarters in the area, and there are also several industrial parks and research centers located in the suburbs. This means that there are plenty of job opportunities for residents, and the economy in the area is generally quite strong.

In addition to the corporate world, the north of Milan is also home to many small businesses and entrepreneurs. From boutique shops to artisanal cafes, there are plenty of opportunities for those looking to start their own business or support local artisans.

Overall, the economy in the north of Milan is diverse and thriving, making it a great place to live and work.
