Are Geraniums Poisonous to Dogs? | News Blog | 2023

A Comprehensive Guide

Geraniums are one of the most popular flowering plants used for decoration in gardens, balconies, and homes. They are easy to grow and come in a variety of colors, making them an attractive choice for pet owners. However, when it comes to dogs, many people wonder whether geraniums are safe or poisonous. In this article, we will explore this topic and provide you with all the necessary information.

What Are Geraniums?

Geraniums, also known as Pelargoniums, are a genus of flowering plants that belong to the Geraniaceae family. They are native to South Africa and are commonly grown in gardens and indoors all over the world. Geraniums come in a wide range of colors, including pink, red, white, orange, and purple. They are easy to care for and can bloom all year round in the right conditions.

Are Geraniums Poisonous to Dogs?

Geraniums are not toxic to dogs, but they can cause mild digestive issues if ingested in large quantities. The leaves and stems of geraniums contain geraniol, which is a fragrant compound that can cause stomach upset, vomiting, and diarrhea in dogs. However, dogs are unlikely to eat large quantities of geraniums as they are not palatable to them. If your dog ingests a small amount of geraniums, they may experience mild symptoms that will resolve on their own in a day or two.

How to Keep Your Dog Safe Around Geraniums?

While geraniums are not toxic to dogs, it is always important to take precautions to keep your furry friend safe. Here are some tips:

  1. Keep geraniums out of reach of your dog.
  2. Train your dog not to eat plants.
  3. Supervise your dog when they are around plants.
  4. Call your veterinarian if you notice any unusual symptoms in your dog.

Conclusion

Geraniums are safe for dogs, but it is always important to take precautions to avoid any potential risks. Keep geraniums out of reach of your dog, train them not to eat plants, and supervise them when they are around plants. If you notice any unusual symptoms in your dog, call your veterinarian immediately. With these simple steps, you can enjoy the beauty of geraniums without any worries.
