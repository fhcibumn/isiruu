When Treating a 3rd-Degree Burn You Should

Carefully Remove Any Clothing or Jewelry

When someone suffers from a 3rd-degree burn, it's important to carefully remove any clothing or jewelry that may be on or near the affected area. This is because clothing or jewelry can retain heat and cause further damage to the skin. If the clothing or jewelry is stuck to the burn, do not try to remove it. Instead, seek medical attention immediately.

Cool the Burn With Running Water

After removing any clothing or jewelry, it's important to cool the burn with running water for at least 10-20 minutes. This will help to reduce pain and swelling, as well as prevent further damage to the skin. Do not use ice or ice water, as this can cause additional damage to the skin.

Cover the Burn With a Clean, Dry Cloth

Once the burn has been cooled, cover it with a clean, dry cloth or bandage. This will help to protect the burn from infection, as well as keep it clean and moist. Do not use any creams, ointments, or butter on the burn, as this can trap heat and cause further damage to the skin.

Seek Medical Attention

It's important to seek medical attention for a 3rd-degree burn, as this type of burn can cause damage to the deeper layers of the skin and may require specialized treatment. Depending on the size and severity of the burn, you may need to be hospitalized for treatment. Do not try to treat a 3rd-degree burn on your own without seeking medical advice.
