Vampire Films On Netflix: A Review Of The Best Bloodsucking Movies In 2023

Netflix has been a go-to platform for vampire enthusiasts for years, offering a wide range of films featuring these nocturnal creatures. From classic thrillers to modern-day love stories, there’s a vampire film for every taste. In this review, we’ll look at the best vampire films on Netflix in 2023, guaranteed to leave you thirsty for more.

The Old Guard

Starring Charlize Theron and KiKi Layne, The Old Guard is a thrilling action movie with a twist. A group of immortal mercenaries led by Theron’s character, Andy, are on a mission to save humanity from a pharmaceutical company with sinister intentions. But when a new member joins the team, they discover a shocking secret that threatens their existence. The Old Guard is a fresh take on the vampire genre, with a unique concept and excellent performances from the cast.

Dracula

Based on the classic novel by Bram Stoker, this three-part miniseries is a must-watch for any vampire fan. Claes Bang delivers a chilling performance as the legendary Dracula, who sets his sights on a young lawyer named Jonathan Harker. As the story unfolds, we see the character’s infamous traits come to life, with plenty of gore and suspense to keep you on the edge of your seat. Dracula is a masterful adaptation that stays true to the source material while adding its own modern twists.

Only Lovers Left Alive

Tilda Swinton and Tom Hiddleston star as two centuries-old vampires who have been in love for centuries. Set against the backdrop of the decaying city of Detroit, the film explores the couple’s relationship as they struggle to find meaning in their eternal existence. Only Lovers Left Alive is a slow-burning, atmospheric film that’s as much a love story as it is a vampire movie. It’s a must-see for anyone looking for something different in the genre.

What We Do in the Shadows

This horror-comedy mockumentary follows a group of vampire flatmates in modern-day New Zealand. Jemaine Clement and Taika Waititi lead the cast as two of the vampires, who are joined by a range of quirky characters, including a vampire who is still stuck in the 19th century and a human familiar who dreams of becoming a vampire himself. What We Do in the Shadows is a hilarious take on the vampire genre, with a clever script and laugh-out-loud moments.
