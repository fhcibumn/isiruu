Welcome to the latest news on the Settrade 100 rankings for the year 2023. As always, these rankings are closely watched by investors and market analysts alike, as they offer valuable insights into the performance of the Thai stock market. Let's take a closer look at some of the key trends and developments.

Top 3 Companies in the Settrade 100 Index

At the top of the list, we have Siam Commercial Bank (SCB), which has retained its position as the number one company in the Settrade 100 index. With a market capitalization of over 1.5 trillion baht, SCB is a heavyweight in the Thai banking sector, and has been a consistent performer over the years. In second place, we have PTT Public Company Limited (PTT), which has moved up from fourth position last year. PTT is a leading energy company in Thailand, with diverse operations in oil and gas exploration, refining, and distribution. Rounding up the top 3 is Bangkok Bank (BBL), which has slipped down from second place last year.

New Entrants in the Top 10

One of the most notable developments in this year's rankings is the emergence of several new companies in the top 10. Central Pattana (CPN), a major developer and operator of shopping malls in Thailand, has jumped from 14th position last year to 6th this year. This is largely due to the company's strong financial performance and expansion into new markets. Another new entrant is Thai Beverage (ThaiBev), which has moved up from 13th position to 9th this year. ThaiBev is a leading producer and distributor of alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages in Thailand and other Southeast Asian countries. Its strong brand portfolio and strategic acquisitions have contributed to its rise in the rankings.

Slipping Down the Rankings

On the other hand, several companies have slipped down the rankings this year, reflecting their weaker performance in the market. One such company is Advanced Info Service (AIS), which has dropped from 5th position last year to 12th this year. AIS is a major mobile network operator in Thailand, but has faced increasing competition in the market, as well as regulatory challenges. Another company that has slipped down the rankings is Charoen Pokphand Foods (CPF), which has moved from 7th position to 11th this year. CPF is a leading producer and exporter of animal feed and processed foods, but has been affected by rising costs and market volatility.

Conclusion

The Settrade 100 rankings for 2023 offer a valuable snapshot of the Thai stock market, and the companies that are driving growth and innovation in various sectors. While there have been some notable changes in the rankings this year, the overall trend is one of stability and resilience, with many companies continuing to deliver strong financial results and expand their operations. As always, investors and analysts will be closely watching these rankings to identify new opportunities and trends in the market.
