What are Charpentes?

Charpentes are wooden or metal structures that support the roof of a building. They consist of a series of interconnected triangles that form a rigid frame. Charpentes are designed to distribute the weight of the roof evenly across the walls of the building, ensuring that the roof remains stable and secure. They also provide support for the roof covering, such as tiles or shingles.

Types of Charpentes

There are several types of charpentes, including traditional, mansard, and hip. Traditional charpentes are the most common type and consist of a horizontal tie beam, two sloping rafters, and a vertical king post. Mansard charpentes, on the other hand, have a steeper slope on one side of the roof and a shallower slope on the other. Hip charpentes are used for roofs with four sloping sides.

Second, they are highly durable and long-lasting, requiring minimal maintenance over time. Finally, charpentes are a cost-effective roofing solution, providing excellent value for money compared to other roofing options.

Maintenance of Charpentes
