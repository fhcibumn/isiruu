Fire Extinguisher CO2 Tank: What Can It Extinguish? - News Blog 2023

Fire extinguishers are crucial in every establishment. They come in various types, and each type is designed to combat specific fires. One type of fire extinguisher that is commonly used is the CO2 tank. But what can it actually extinguish? In this article, we'll discuss the different types of fires that the CO2 tank can extinguish.

Class B Fires

The CO2 tank is primarily used to extinguish Class B fires, which involve flammable liquids and gases. These fires can occur in industrial settings, laboratories, and even in kitchens. The CO2 tank works by displacing the oxygen in the air, thereby smothering the fire. It also cools down the fuel, preventing it from reigniting.

Class C Fires

Aside from Class B fires, the CO2 tank can also extinguish Class C fires. These fires involve electrical equipment, such as computers, servers, and other electronics. The CO2 tank is safe to use on these fires because it does not conduct electricity. It is important to note, however, that the power source should be turned off before attempting to extinguish the fire.

Class K Fires

Class K fires are fires that involve cooking oils and fats. These fires are common in commercial kitchens and restaurants. The CO2 tank can also be used to extinguish Class K fires, but it is not as effective as other types of fire extinguishers, such as wet chemical extinguishers. It is important to have the right type of fire extinguisher in the kitchen to ensure maximum safety.

Class A Fires

Class A fires involve ordinary combustibles such as wood, paper, and cloth. The CO2 tank can be used to extinguish Class A fires, but it is not recommended. This is because the CO2 tank does not cool down the fuel, which means that the fire may reignite. It is better to use water or foam extinguishers for Class A fires.

In Conclusion

The CO2 tank is a versatile fire extinguisher that can be used on Class B, C, and K fires. It is important to understand the different types of fires and the appropriate fire extinguisher to use for each type. Remember to always follow safety protocols and evacuate the area if the fire is too big to handle. Stay safe!
