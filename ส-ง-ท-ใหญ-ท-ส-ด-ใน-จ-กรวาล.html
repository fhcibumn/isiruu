What is the biggest thing in the universe? This question has lingered in the minds of many curious individuals for centuries. The universe is a vast expanse, and it is home to some of the most massive objects in existence. From stars to galaxies, let's explore some of the biggest things in the universe.

Stars

Stars are massive celestial bodies that are held together by gravity and emit light and heat. They are the building blocks of the universe and come in different sizes, but the biggest stars are called hypergiants. These stars are incredibly rare and have a mass of over 100 times that of our sun. One of the most massive stars ever discovered is the R136a1, which has a mass of over 300 times that of our sun. It is located in the Tarantula Nebula, which is about 160,000 light-years away from Earth.

Black Holes

Black holes are some of the most intriguing objects in the universe. They are formed when massive stars collapse under their own gravity, creating a region in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. The largest black hole discovered so far is the TON 618, which has a mass estimated to be about 66 billion times that of our sun. It is located about 10.4 billion light-years away from Earth.

Galaxies

A galaxy is a massive collection of stars, dust, and gas held together by gravity. They come in different shapes and sizes, but the biggest galaxies are called giant elliptical galaxies. These galaxies can contain trillions of stars and have a mass of over 100 trillion times that of our sun. The biggest galaxy discovered so far is the IC 1101, which has a diameter of about 6 million light-years and a mass estimated to be over 100 trillion times that of our sun.

Clusters of Galaxies

A cluster of galaxies is a collection of galaxies held together by gravity. They are the largest structures in the universe and can contain hundreds or thousands of galaxies. The biggest cluster of galaxies discovered so far is the El Gordo, which is a merging cluster located about 7 billion light-years away from Earth. It has a mass estimated to be about 3 quadrillion times that of our sun.
