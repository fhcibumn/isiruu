Pain in Back of Knee When Straightening Leg - Causes and Treatments

Cause 1: Hamstring Injuries

If you experience pain in the back of your knee when straightening your leg, hamstring injuries could be the culprit. The hamstring muscles run from the hip to the back of the knee and help to bend the knee and extend the hip. A hamstring injury can result in pain in the back of the knee, especially when you try to straighten your leg. Common causes of hamstring injuries include sports-related activities, previous injury, and overuse.

Treatment for hamstring injuries often includes rest, ice, compression, and elevation (RICE). Physical therapy may also be recommended to help strengthen the hamstring muscles and prevent future injuries. In severe cases, surgery may be necessary.

Cause 2: Meniscus Tears

Another potential cause of pain in the back of the knee when straightening your leg is a meniscus tear. The meniscus is a piece of cartilage in the knee joint that helps to cushion and stabilize the joint. When the meniscus is torn, it can cause pain, swelling, and stiffness in the knee.

Treatment for meniscus tears may include rest, ice, compression, and elevation (RICE). Physical therapy may also be recommended to help strengthen the muscles around the knee and improve flexibility. In some cases, surgery may be necessary to repair or remove the torn meniscus.

Cause 3: Arthritis

Arthritis is a common condition that can cause pain in the back of the knee when straightening your leg. Osteoarthritis, in particular, can cause the cartilage in the knee joint to wear down over time, leading to pain and stiffness. Rheumatoid arthritis, on the other hand, is an autoimmune disorder that can cause inflammation in the knee joint.

Treatment for arthritis may include medications, physical therapy, and lifestyle changes such as losing weight or modifying activities. In severe cases, surgery may be necessary to replace the damaged joint.

Cause 4: Popliteus Tendinitis

Popliteus tendinitis is a condition that occurs when the popliteus tendon, which runs along the back of the knee, becomes inflamed. This can cause pain in the back of the knee, especially when straightening the leg.

Treatment for popliteus tendinitis may include rest, ice, compression, and elevation (RICE). Physical therapy may also be recommended to help strengthen the muscles around the knee and improve flexibility. In severe cases, surgery may be necessary.
