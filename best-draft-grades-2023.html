Best Draft Grades 2023

The 2023 NFL Draft has come and gone, leaving fans and analysts alike wondering which teams came out on top. With a plethora of talented prospects available, it's always interesting to see how each team's draft grade stacks up against the rest. Here are some of the best draft grades of 2023:

1. Dallas Cowboys

The Dallas Cowboys had a strong draft in 2023, starting with their first overall pick, defensive end Derek Ellis. Ellis is a dominant pass rusher who will bring a much-needed boost to the Cowboys' defense. The Cowboys also picked up offensive tackle Liam Thompson in the second round, who has the potential to become a long-term starter for the team. With a solid draft from top to bottom, the Cowboys earned an A grade from many analysts.

2. Jacksonville Jaguars

The Jacksonville Jaguars had the first overall pick in the 2023 draft, and they used it wisely by selecting quarterback Ryan Johnson. Johnson is a talented passer who has the potential to become a franchise quarterback for the Jaguars. The Jaguars also picked up several other talented players, including cornerback Marcus Green and offensive tackle Tyler Martinez. With a strong draft that addressed several key needs, the Jaguars earned an A grade from many analysts.

3. New York Giants

The New York Giants had a solid draft in 2023, starting with their first-round pick, wide receiver Xavier James. James is a dynamic playmaker who will give the Giants' offense a much-needed boost. The Giants also picked up several other talented players, including linebacker Tyler Nguyen and defensive end Kevin Smith. With a strong draft that addressed several key needs, the Giants earned a B+ grade from many analysts.

4. Los Angeles Chargers

The Los Angeles Chargers had a strong draft in 2023, starting with their first-round pick, cornerback Kyle Wilson. Wilson is a shutdown corner who will instantly improve the Chargers' secondary. The Chargers also picked up several other talented players, including offensive tackle Alex Rodriguez and tight end Anthony Taylor. With a solid draft that addressed several key needs, the Chargers earned a B+ grade from many analysts.
