Video games have always been known for their stunning graphics, immersive gameplay, and engaging storylines. But what about their soundtracks? For years, video game composers have been creating amazing music that perfectly captures the essence of the games they accompany. And in 2023, fans from around the world have come together to vote on the best video game songs of the year.

The competition has been fierce, with countless incredible tracks vying for the top spot. But which ones made the cut? Let's take a closer look at some of the most popular video game songs of 2023:

The Last of Us Part III: "Through the Valley"

The Last of Us Part III is one of the most highly anticipated games of the year, and its soundtrack does not disappoint. "Through the Valley" by Shawn James perfectly captures the post-apocalyptic atmosphere of the game, with haunting vocals and a somber guitar melody. Fans have been raving about this song since it was first released, and it's no wonder it's one of the top contenders for best video game song of the year.

Deathloop: "Deja Vu"

Deathloop is a new game that has taken the gaming world by storm, and its soundtrack is no exception. "Deja Vu" by Sencit Music perfectly captures the game's time-bending mechanics, with a fast-paced beat and a sense of urgency that keeps players on the edge of their seats. Fans have been humming this tune for months, and it's no wonder it's one of the top contenders for best video game song of the year.

Final Fantasy XVI: "Awakening"

Final Fantasy XVI is the latest installment in the beloved Final Fantasy franchise, and its soundtrack is nothing short of epic. "Awakening" by Masashi Hamauzu perfectly captures the grandeur and adventure of the game, with soaring strings and a triumphant melody. Fans have been eagerly anticipating this game for years, and this incredible song has only added to the hype.

Elden Ring: "The Tarnished"

Elden Ring is a new game from the minds behind Dark Souls and Bloodborne, and its soundtrack is just as haunting and atmospheric as its predecessors. "The Tarnished" by Yuka Kitamura perfectly captures the game's dark and mysterious world, with eerie vocals and a haunting melody. Fans have been eagerly awaiting this game for years, and this incredible song has only added to the anticipation.

These are just a few of the incredible video game songs that have been nominated for best of the year. Which one will take home the top prize? Only time will tell. But one thing is for sure: video game music is more than just background noise. It's an integral part of the gaming experience, and these amazing songs prove it.
