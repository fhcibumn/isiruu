The Average Temperature In Phoenix In September

Phoenix, Arizona is known for its scorching hot summers and mild winters. As the summer months begin to wind down, many residents and tourists alike may be wondering what kind of weather they can expect in September. In this article, we'll take a closer look at the average temperature in Phoenix during this time of year and what it means for those planning a trip or living in the area.

What is the Average Temperature in September?

According to data from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), the average temperature in Phoenix in September is around 92 degrees Fahrenheit (33 degrees Celsius). However, it's important to note that this is just an average and temperatures can vary significantly from day to day.

September is still considered part of the summer season in Phoenix, so it's not uncommon to experience high temperatures that can reach into the triple digits. However, as the month progresses, temperatures tend to gradually decrease, making it a more comfortable time to be outdoors.

What Factors Affect the Temperature in September?

There are several factors that can affect the temperature in Phoenix during September. One of the biggest factors is the amount of rainfall the area receives. In general, Phoenix is a very dry city, but September tends to be one of the wetter months of the year. When there is more rainfall, it can help to cool the temperature down, making it more comfortable for those outdoors.

Another factor that can affect the temperature is the amount of cloud cover. When there are more clouds in the sky, it can help to block out some of the sun's rays, which can also help to lower the temperature. However, if there are too many clouds, it can make the air feel muggy and uncomfortable.

What Should You Wear in September?

When it comes to dressing for the weather in Phoenix during September, it's important to keep in mind that temperatures can still be quite warm, especially during the daytime. Light, breathable fabrics such as cotton and linen are good choices, as they can help to keep you cool and comfortable. It's also a good idea to wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun's strong rays.

As the evening temperatures start to cool down, it's a good idea to bring a light jacket or sweater with you, as it can get chilly after the sun goes down. However, it's important to note that even though the temperatures may be cooler, the sun can still be strong, so be sure to continue to wear sunscreen and protect your skin.

What Activities Can You Do in September?

Despite the warm temperatures, there are still plenty of activities to enjoy in Phoenix during September. Many of the city's outdoor attractions, such as hiking trails and botanical gardens, are still open and can be enjoyed during the cooler parts of the day. The city also hosts a variety of festivals and events throughout the month, including the Arizona State Fair and the Phoenix Greek Festival.

For those who prefer indoor activities, there are also a number of museums and galleries to explore, as well as shopping centers and restaurants to enjoy.
